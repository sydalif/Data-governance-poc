Annual Data Governance Briefing

This briefing provides an overview of key concepts from the DAMA-DMBOK (Data Management Body of Knowledge), tailored to our agency's role in administering business registration in Singapore. Understanding and applying these principles ensures our data is accurate, secure, and responsibly used to support government operations and public trust.

Data Governance (General Knowledge)

Data governance is the foundation for managing data assets responsibly. It defines policies, roles, and responsibilities to ensure data is properly handled throughout its lifecycle.

  • Assigns accountability for data quality and protection
  • Sets clear standards and procedures for managing data
  • Ensures compliance with Singapore laws such as PDPA
  • Supports transparency, oversight, and auditability
"Effective data governance enables confident, timely decisions while safeguarding sensitive information."

Data Quality

Reliable business registration depends on data that is accurate, complete, and timely. The four key dimensions of data quality are:

  • Accuracy: Reflects true values (e.g., business names, dates)
  • Completeness: No missing fields in required data
  • Consistency: Data is uniform across systems
  • Timeliness: Information is updated promptly

All staff are expected to follow validation and correction processes, and report any discrepancies to ensure high data quality is maintained.

Data Protection

As public officers, we have a duty under the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) to protect sensitive information collected from businesses and individuals.

  • Access to data is limited to authorised personnel only
  • Use secure, government-approved platforms for handling data
  • Report suspected data breaches immediately to the Data Protection Officer
  • Use data only for its stated and legal purpose
  • Dispose of data securely when no longer needed
"Data protection safeguards not just information, but also public confidence in government institutions."

Data Access and Exploitation (Fusion)

We grant access to data based on the principle of least privilege — access only to the data you need to do your job.

Data exploitation and fusion refer to responsible use and combining of data from different sources for insight and efficiency.

  • Access control is reviewed and logged regularly
  • Fusion of datasets (e.g., registration + tax info) must be justified and secure
  • Use analytics to improve policies and identify fraud
  • Ensure ethical and legal use of combined data

Example: Analyzing business trends by combining registration data with economic indicators to inform policy decisions.

Key Staff Responsibilities

  • Follow documented procedures to maintain data integrity
  • Adhere strictly to data protection policies
  • Report any suspicious or incorrect data handling
  • Participate in annual data governance training and testing
  • Use only government-issued systems for work-related data

Each staff member is a data steward — responsible for ensuring trust and accuracy in the agency’s data management practices.
